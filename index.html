Urea Cycle Glossary

Key Terms

Urea Cycle
A series of biochemical reactions that convert toxic ammonia (NH3) to urea for safe excretion. Takes place partly in mitochondria and partly in cytosol.
Mitochondria
The "powerhouse" of the cell where the first steps of the urea cycle occur. Contains enzymes CPS1 and OTC.
Cytosol
The liquid inside cells (but outside organelles) where the final steps of the urea cycle take place.
Ammonia (NH3)
A toxic waste product from protein breakdown that must be converted to urea for safe disposal.
Carbamoyl Phosphate
First intermediate in the urea cycle, made from NH3, CO2, and 2 ATP by enzyme CPS1.
Ornithine
An amino acid that carries the cycle forward and is regenerated at the end.
Citrulline
Product of ornithine + carbamoyl phosphate, transported from mitochondria to cytosol.
Argininosuccinate
Intermediate formed from citrulline + aspartate in the cytosol by enzyme ASS.
Arginine
Precursor to urea, split by arginase to produce urea and regenerate ornithine.
Fumarate
Byproduct that enters the citric acid cycle, converted to malate by fumarase.
Urea
Final non-toxic product containing two nitrogen atoms, safely excreted in urine.

Key Enzymes

CPS1 (Carbamoyl Phosphate Synthetase I)
First enzyme, combines NH3 + CO2 + 2ATP to make carbamoyl phosphate. Requires N-acetylglutamate.
OTC (Ornithine Transcarbamylase)
Combines ornithine + carbamoyl phosphate to make citrulline in mitochondria.
ASS (Argininosuccinate Synthetase)
Combines citrulline + aspartate + ATP to make argininosuccinate in cytosol.
ASL (Argininosuccinate Lyase)
Splits argininosuccinate into arginine + fumarate.
ARG1 (Arginase)
Final enzyme, splits arginine into urea + ornithine.
